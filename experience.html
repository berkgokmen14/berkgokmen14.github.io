Cornell Computer Systems Lab - Undergraduate Researcher

Ithaca, New York

September 2023 - May 2025

Experience Description:

I worked with Professor Zhiru Zhang for two years on two research projects: AMC and EqMap. Throughout my time at the lab, I worked closely with PhD student Mathew Hoffmann and four other undergraduate researchers in weekly meetings to review code, share feedback, and brainstorm new ideas.

AMC addresses the memory customization challenges in high-level synthesis (HLS). HLS relies on user directives to achieve efficient memory structures, requiring expertise and making it a less end-to-end solution. We developed a novel MLIR intermediate representation and compiler to be able to represent complex memory structures. AMC leverages this abstraction, termed “MemTL,” and supports a Python-based DSL to describe algorithms with less user concern for memory subsystems. During my time at the lab, I added support for arithmetic operations and wrote a loop flattening optimization pass leading to a 30-40% cycle reduction for deep nested loops. I also heavily worked on the verification of the compiler by writing unit tests for optimization passes, conducting performance tests against Vivado, and performing application tests. My work here led to a third-author paper submission for FPGA '25!

EqMap is an ongoing project that addresses the area optimization problem for FPGAs: Given a Verilog file or a list of nets, what is the most efficient way to pack the netlist into LUTs? EqMap uses E-graphs, a superoptimization technique, to search for the optimal solution. My work here led to a second-author paper submission for ICCAD '25!
